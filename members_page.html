Lab Coordinator:

Rui A. Lima (RL)

PhD in Engineering (Bioengineering and Robotics Dep., Tohoku Univ., Japan, 2007)

MSc(Eng) in Envir. and Energy Eng. (Chemical & Process Eng. Dep., Sheffield Univ., U.K., 2000)

Dipl. Eng. in Mechanical Engineering (Minho Univ.,1998).


Currently, RL is an Associate Professor at the Dep. of Mechanical Engineering (DME), Minho Univ. (UMinho) and an integrated member at the Mechanical Engineering and Resource Sustainability Center (MEtRICs).


He is the main research is mostly centered in microfluidics, nanofluidics, organs-on-a-chip and biofluids. He has a h-index=33, more than 170 scientific papers (> 3500 citations), 5 patents (1 international) and has supervised 1 postdoc fellow, 11 PhD thesis (5 finished) and more than 50 Master thesis. He has participated in more than 40 projects and has been Principal Investigator (PI) in 8 of them.


RL lectures several courses in Engineering. He is responsible for the Microtechnologies and Biofluids Lab. and Lab4DEM, both from DME/UMinho. Currently, he is the Director of the Master in Micro/Nano Technologies and in the past he was the Director of the Doctoral program "Leaders for Technological Industries" (MIT Portugal program) and Master in Biomedical Technologies (IPB).


RL belongs to the editorial board of several journals in the microfluidics and engineering field such as: Micromachines, Advances in Mechanical Eng. and WS Annual Review of Biomechanics. He has been a member of several societies such as the European and Portuguese Society of Biomechanics. Additionally, he has been a reviewer for several journals such as: Nature Communications, Small, Biomicrofluidics, Microfluidics and Nanofluidics, and Micromachines.


During the years he has collaborated with several International Institutions such as: Tohoku Univ. (Japan), Extremadura Univ.(Spain), Tokyo Medical and Dental Univ.(Japan), Harvard Medical School (USA), MIT (USA), Strathclyde Univ.(UK) and Bristol Univ.(UK).
