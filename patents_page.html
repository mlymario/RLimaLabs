PROTOTYPES:

Some selected prototypes and clinical tools are the following:

1. CSD-chip: A Continuous flow microfluidic device for RBC Separation and Deformability assessment, first proposed in 2011 and in 2019 was successfully tested with fifteen diabetic type II patients from Bragança Hospital, Portugal.

https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mne.2022.100149




2. RBChyperbolic-chip: A microfluidic device to assess RBCs deformability under a controlled extensional flow, first proposed in 2012 and successfully tested with 20 end-stage kidney disease patients from Gondomar Hemodialysis Clinic, Portugal. The effect of nanocarriers on the RBCs mechanical deformability was also assessed. It was proved that this clinical tool has a higher sensitivity in comparison with traditional hemolysis methods.

https://doi.org/10.3390/mi10100645

https://doi.org/10.1007/s11051-016-3498-7
